Harriett Tubman

1822 - 1913

Harriet Tubman’s Early Life

Born into slavery in Maryland, Harriet Tubman’s early life was marked by struggle and unimaginable hardship. Despite these challenges, even as a young girl, she displayed an indomitable spirit and a yearning for freedom. Her experiences during this time laid the foundation for the fearless leader she would become.

Escape to Freedom

After securing her own freedom, she made it her mission to go back, again and again, leading over 70 enslaved people to safety via the Underground Railroad. Her courage defied the odds, and she became known as the ‘Moses of her people,’ lighting the way to freedom for countless others.

The Underground Railroad

As a conductor on the Underground Railroad, Harriet Tubman was a beacon of hope. With her deep understanding of the land, she led mission after mission, never losing a single ‘passenger.’ Harriet believed in freedom above all else. Her life was constantly at risk, but she once said, ‘I never ran my train off the track, and I never lost a passenger.

Her Role in the Civil War

During the Civil War, Harriet’s fight for freedom took on new dimensions. She worked as a nurse, cook, and even a spy for the Union Army, using her knowledge of the terrain to gather intelligence. Her courage was on full display when she led an armed raid on Combahee River plantations, liberating more than 700 enslaved people in one night.

Her Later Years

Even after the war, Harriet Tubman’s fight for justice was far from over. She was a vocal advocate for women’s suffrage and dedicated her later years to caring for the elderly and those in need. Her spirit of compassion and service never waned, and she remained a tireless advocate for equality until her passing in 1913.

Legacy and Impact

Harriet Tubman’s legacy is not confined to the history books; it lives on in every act of courage, in every fight for justice. Her name stands as a symbol of the power one person can have to change the world. Her story continues to be told, ensuring that her heroism and relentless pursuit of freedom are never forgotten.

“I go away to prepare a place for you, that where I am you also may be” - Harriet Tubman
